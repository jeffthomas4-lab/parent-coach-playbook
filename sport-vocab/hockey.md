# Hockey — sport vocabulary

The puck is not a ball. The rink is not a court. Body-checking is restricted by age — see USA Hockey rules.

## The rink

- Correct: rink, ice, blue line, red line (center line), goal line, faceoff dot, faceoff circle, neutral zone, offensive zone, defensive zone, attacking zone, crease, slot, point (the area near the blue line where defensemen play), the boards, the glass, the corners, the bench, the penalty box
- Wrong for this sport: pitch, diamond, court, gridiron, end zone, paint

## Players and positions

- Forwards: center (C), left wing (LW), right wing (RW)
- Defensemen: left defenseman (LD), right defenseman (RD)
- Goalie (or goaltender, or netminder)
- 5 skaters plus a goalie on the ice at full strength (6 total)
- Wrong for this sport: pitcher, striker, midfielder, point guard, lineman, libero, setter

## Action verbs

- Skate, pass, shoot, deke, deflect, tip, dump, chip, pinch, backcheck, forecheck, breakout, cycle, screen, set up, one-time, drop pass, saucer pass
- Stop the puck: save (goalie), poke check, stick check, body check (subject to age rules), block (a shot)
- Pass types: tape-to-tape, saucer pass, stretch pass, drop pass
- Shot types: wrist shot, slap shot, snap shot, backhand, one-timer, tip-in
- Wrong for this sport: dribble, dunk, spike, dig, snap, tackle, cradle, hand off

## Body-checking note

- USA Hockey allows body-checking starting at the 14U level (Bantam) in most divisions. At younger levels (12U Peewee and under), body-checking is illegal — checking is limited to angling, stick-on-stick, and body positioning. In girls' hockey, body-checking is not part of the game at any level.
- A drill piece for younger ages should not include body-check actions. If it does, that's a sport-language fail and a flag-sensitive-topic concern.
- Cite USA Hockey when rules are involved.

## Scoring

- A team scores a goal when the entire puck crosses the goal line into the net.
- An assist is credited to up to two teammates who touched the puck on the goal-scoring possession. A point in hockey means a goal or an assist (a "point" is a stat, not a unit of score).
- Wrong for this sport: run, basket, touchdown, kill. Don't say "scored a point" — the goal is what counts.

## Game structure

- Period (three of them), intermission, regulation, overtime, shootout, faceoff, line change, shift, power play, penalty kill, even strength, short-handed
- Period length varies by age — cite the league.
- Wrong for this sport: inning, quarter, half (hockey doesn't use halves), set, match, frame, down

## Equipment

- Skates, stick, helmet (with cage or visor by age — cite USA Hockey), shoulder pads, elbow pads, gloves, hockey pants, shin guards, jersey, mouthguard (required at most levels), neck guard, jock or jill, puck
- Goalie has different equipment: leg pads, blocker, catcher (glove), chest protector, mask, stick (different shape)
- Wrong for this sport: bat, racquet, ball (it's a puck), cleats, flag belt

## Cross-sport pitfalls (most common slips)

- "Ball" — wrong. Puck.
- "Scored a touchdown" or "scored a basket" — wrong. Scored a goal.
- "Tackled the player" — wrong. Body-checked, or angled off, or pinned to the boards.
- "The pitch" — wrong as a rink reference. The rink, or the ice.
- "The forward kicked the puck into the goal" — kicking is not allowed for goal-scoring under most rule sets. Be careful: a goal can sometimes be deflected off a skate, but a deliberate kick for a goal is disallowed. Cite the rule before writing this.
- Confusing hockey "save" (goalie stops the puck) with baseball "save" (relief pitcher closes a game). Two different sports, two different terms.
