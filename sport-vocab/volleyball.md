# Volleyball — sport vocabulary

The terminology is precise. "Set," "spike," and "kill" all mean specific things and should not be swapped.

## The court

- Correct: court, net, antenna, attack line (the 10-foot line, sometimes called the 3-meter line), front row, back row, sideline, end line, service line, service area, net pole or standard
- The court is divided in half by the net. Each side is 9 by 9 meters.
- Wrong for this sport: pitch, diamond, gridiron, rink, end zone, lane

## Players and positions

- Outside hitter (OH), opposite (OPP, also called right-side hitter), middle blocker (MB), setter (S), libero (L — wears a different-colored jersey, plays back row only, can't attack above the net), defensive specialist (DS)
- 6 players on the court at once
- Wrong for this sport: pitcher, striker, point guard, lineman, goalie

## Action verbs

- Serve, receive (also called pass — in volleyball, "pass" means the first contact off a serve or attack), set, attack (or hit, or spike — these are interchangeable for the third contact), block, dig (defensive contact off an attack), cover, transition, rotate, tip (a soft, controlled touchdown of the ball with the fingertips into open court), roll shot
- Serve types: float serve, jump serve, jump float, topspin
- Wrong for this sport: shoot, dribble, dunk, snap, tackle, body-check, cradle

## Scoring

- A team scores a point when the ball lands in the opponent's court, the opponent commits a fault, or the opponent fails to return the ball legally. Rally scoring — every rally produces a point.
- A kill is an attack that wins the rally outright (lands in, or is unreturnable). A kill is a stat, not a separate scoring event.
- An ace is a serve that wins the rally outright (lands in the opponent's court untouched, or is shanked unplayably).
- Wrong for this sport: run, goal, basket, touchdown. ("Score a basket" in a volleyball piece is a tell that the writer is in the wrong sport.)

## Game structure

- Rally, set (one segment of the match — typically played to 25, win by 2; the deciding 5th set is played to 15), match (best of 3 or best of 5 sets)
- "Set" has two meanings in volleyball: (1) the second contact of a possession, made by the setter; (2) one segment of the match. Context disambiguates. In a drill piece, prefer the verb "set" and the noun "set" only when context is clear.
- Wrong for this sport: inning, quarter, period, frame, down, half

## Equipment

- Volleyball, knee pads, court shoes (volleyball-specific shoes have gum rubber soles for grip), ankle braces (common at higher levels)
- Wrong for this sport: bat, glove, stick, racquet, helmet, pads (other than knee pads), cleats

## Cross-sport pitfalls (most common slips)

- "Shot the ball over the net" — wrong. Hit, attacked, or sent the ball over.
- "Scored a goal" — wrong. Scored a point. Or, more specifically, "got a kill."
- "The pitch" — wrong as a court reference. The court.
- "Striker" — wrong. Outside hitter or opposite.
- "Period" or "quarter" — wrong. Sets.
- Confusing "set" the verb (the second contact) with "set" the noun (the match segment). Read your draft and make sure both meanings are clear from context.
- "Spike home run" or "spike touchdown" — wrong. A spike that wins the rally is a kill.
- "Save" (as in goalie save) — wrong. The defensive contact that keeps a ball alive is a dig.
