# Baseball — sport vocabulary

Use these terms. Avoid the "wrong for this sport" terms unless the piece is explicitly comparing sports.

## The field

- Correct: diamond, infield, outfield, foul territory, fair territory, foul line, foul pole, warning track, mound, plate (or home plate), batter's box, on-deck circle, dugout, bullpen, base path
- Bases: first base, second base, third base, home plate. Not "home base."
- Wrong for this sport: pitch (= soccer field), court, gridiron, end zone, rink, net

## Players and positions

- Pitcher, catcher, first baseman, second baseman, shortstop, third baseman, left fielder, center fielder, right fielder
- The kid at the plate is the batter or hitter
- The kid running between bases is the runner or baserunner
- Wrong for this sport: striker, midfielder, attacker, defender, forward, goalie, lineman, libero, setter

## Action verbs

- Pitch (the throw from mound to plate), throw (any other throw), hit, swing, field, catch, scoop, tag, slide, steal, lead off, bunt, sacrifice
- Out types: ground out, fly out, line out, pop out, force out, tag out, strikeout
- Pitches: fastball, change-up, curveball, slider, splitter, knuckleball
- Wrong for this sport: shoot (a basketball/soccer term), kick, dribble, dunk, spike, dig, body-check, tackle, deke

## Scoring

- A team scores a run when a baserunner crosses home plate.
- Hits: single, double, triple, home run
- Stats nouns: RBI (run batted in), hit, walk (BB), strikeout (K), error
- Wrong for this sport: goal, point (a "point" is a non-baseball scoring noun — never say "scored a point" in baseball), basket, touchdown, kill, ace (an "ace" in baseball means the team's top starting pitcher, not a winning serve)

## Game structure

- Inning (top half, bottom half), at-bat, count (balls and strikes), three outs end the half-inning, nine innings in a regulation game (varies for youth — Little League uses six innings)
- Wrong for this sport: quarter, half, period, set, match, frame (frame is occasional baseball slang for inning, but write "inning")

## Equipment

- Bat, glove, mitt (catcher's and first baseman's), helmet, batting helmet, cleats, baseball
- "Ball" is fine. Slang like "pill" or "rock" or "horsehide" doesn't belong in beginner-parent prose.
- Wrong for this sport: stick, racquet, club, puck

## Cross-sport pitfalls (most common slips)

- "Scored a goal" — wrong. Use "scored a run."
- "Shot toward second" — wrong. Use "ran toward second" or "sprinted to second."
- "The pitch was 40 yards by 70 yards" — wrong. The field/diamond, not the pitch.
- "Tackled the runner at second" — wrong. Tagged the runner, or applied the tag.
- "Goalie" — there is no goalie in baseball. The catcher catches; the catcher does not "guard the goal."
