# Soccer — sport vocabulary

US writers often slip into football terms (the wrong football). Stay disciplined.

## The field

- Correct: pitch, field, halfway line, center circle, penalty area (or "the box"), goal area (or "the six"), goal, goalmouth, post, crossbar, corner, corner arc, touchline, goal line, technical area
- Wrong for this sport: diamond, court, gridiron, end zone, rink, lane, paint

## Players and positions

- Goalkeeper (GK or keeper), defenders (center back / CB, fullback / FB, wingback / WB), midfielders (center mid / CM, defensive mid / DM, attacking mid / AM, winger), forwards or strikers (CF, ST), attacker
- The kid with the ball is the attacker or the player on the ball, the defender is the player marking
- Wrong for this sport: pitcher, catcher, shortstop, lineman, point guard, libero, setter

## Action verbs

- Dribble, pass, receive, trap, shoot, head (or "head the ball"), tackle (in soccer this means challenging for the ball with the foot, NOT a body tackle), slide tackle, mark, press, recover, cross, chip, volley, switch the field
- Set pieces: corner kick, throw-in, free kick, penalty kick, goal kick, kickoff
- Wrong for this sport: shoot a basket, dunk, snap, hand off, body-check, deke, cradle, spike, dig

## Scoring

- A team scores a goal when the entire ball crosses the goal line between the posts and under the bar.
- Wrong for this sport: run, point (in soccer "points" only refer to standings — three for a win, one for a draw — never to a single goal), basket, touchdown, kill

## Game structure

- Match (not "game" in formal usage, though "game" is common parent-speak and acceptable), half (two halves), halftime, stoppage time (or "added time"), extra time, penalty shootout
- Adult halves are 45 minutes; youth varies. Cite US Soccer / state association when the duration matters.
- Wrong for this sport: inning, quarter, period, set, frame, down

## Equipment

- Ball (size 3 ages 8 and under, size 4 ages 9–12, size 5 ages 13+), shin guards (required), cleats (metal cleats banned in youth play), goalkeeper gloves
- Wrong for this sport: stick, racquet, helmet (no helmets in soccer), pads

## Cross-sport pitfalls (most common slips)

- "Scored a touchdown" — wrong. A goal.
- "Carrying the ball downfield" — wrong. Dribbling, or moving the ball, or running with the ball at the feet.
- "Shot a basket" or "made a basket" — wrong. Shot on goal, took a shot, or shot.
- "Tackled the player" with body contact implied — soccer tackles are at the ball with the foot. Body-tackling a player is a foul. Be precise.
- "Net" is fine for the goal net. Don't write "shot the ball into the basket's net."
- "Field" and "pitch" are interchangeable in American writing; both are correct. Pick one and stay consistent inside a piece.
