# Lacrosse — sport vocabulary

Boys' lacrosse and girls' lacrosse are different sports in important ways: equipment, contact rules, stick design, even the field markings and player counts. Read the section that matches the piece's tag (`lacrosse-boys`, `lacrosse-girls`, or `lacrosse` if generic).

## The field (both)

- Correct: field, attack zone, defensive zone, midfield line, restraining line, the box (substitution area), the wing area, alley, goal, crease, faceoff X (boys) or center circle (girls), endline, sideline, goal line extended (GLE)
- Wrong for this sport: pitch (= soccer), diamond, court, gridiron, rink

## Players (boys' lacrosse)

- 10 on the field at a time: 3 attackmen, 3 midfielders (middies), 3 defensemen, 1 goalie
- Defensemen carry long poles (long sticks); attackmen and most middies carry short sticks; one long-stick midfielder (LSM) is allowed
- Wrong for this sport: pitcher, striker, point guard, lineman

## Players (girls' lacrosse)

- 12 on the field at a time: 1 goalie, 3 attackers, 5 midfielders, 3 defenders. Some leagues use slightly different formations.
- All sticks are the same general type (no long poles in girls'). Goalie stick is different.
- Wrong for this sport: same as boys'

## Action verbs (both)

- Cradle (continuous wrist motion to keep the ball in the stick), pass, catch, scoop (ground ball), shoot, dodge, ride, clear, set picks
- Wrong for this sport: dribble (you don't dribble a lacrosse ball), shoot a basket, snap, tackle (in girls' especially), spike, dig, body-check (in girls'), deke, hand off

## Action verbs (boys' specific)

- Body-check (legal between certain players within rules — cite US Lacrosse), stick-check (slap, poke, lift), faceoff (the play that begins each quarter and after each goal — two players at the X)
- Body contact between attackers and defenders is part of the boys' game, governed by rules on angle and force.

## Action verbs (girls' specific)

- Stick-check only — no body contact. Stick-checks must be controlled and follow strict rules around the head and the sphere of safety.
- Draw (the play that begins each half and after each goal — two players at center, sticks back-to-back, ball lifted on the whistle)
- Cross-sport pitfall: writing "the defender body-checked the attacker" in a girls' lacrosse piece is wrong AND a safety issue. Body contact is not part of the girls' game.

## Scoring

- A team scores a goal when the entire ball crosses the goal line into the net.
- Stats: goal, assist, ground ball (GB), caused turnover (CT), save (goalie)
- Wrong for this sport: run, point (a "point" is goal + assist, like hockey — not a unit of score), basket, touchdown, kill

## Game structure

- Boys': four quarters (length varies by age — cite). Faceoff at start and after each goal.
- Girls': two halves (length varies by age — cite). Draw at start and after each goal.
- Wrong for this sport: inning, set, match, down, frame

## Equipment

**Boys':**
- Stick (short stick or long pole), helmet with face mask, mouthguard, shoulder pads, elbow pads, gloves, athletic cup, cleats, ball
- Goalie has additional chest protector and a larger goalie stick.

**Girls':**
- Stick (one design — not segmented short/long like boys'), goggles (eyewear required), mouthguard, gloves (optional), cleats, ball
- Helmets are not required in most girls' rule sets at the youth level (this is changing — cite USA Lacrosse for the current rule).
- Goalie has a helmet, chest protector, leg pads, and a goalie stick.

- Wrong for this sport: bat, racquet, puck, flag belt

## Cross-sport pitfalls (most common slips)

- "Body-checked" in a girls' piece — wrong, and a safety flag.
- "Scored a touchdown" — wrong. A goal.
- "Dribbled past the defender" — wrong. Cradled past, or dodged past, or beat the defender.
- "The puck" — wrong. The ball.
- "The pitch" — wrong as a field reference. The field.
- Mixing boys' and girls' rules in a generic-tagged piece. Better to tag the piece `lacrosse-boys` or `lacrosse-girls` and write to one rule set.
- Calling a faceoff a draw (or vice versa). Faceoff is boys'; draw is girls'.
