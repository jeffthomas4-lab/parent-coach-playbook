# Softball — sport vocabulary

Same diamond shape as baseball, but the pitching motion, base distances, and ball are different. Treat softball as its own sport, not "baseball with a bigger ball."

## The field

- Correct: diamond, infield, outfield, foul territory, fair territory, foul line, mound (or pitching circle), pitching rubber, plate, batter's box, on-deck circle, dugout
- Base paths in fastpitch are 60 feet (vs. 90 in baseball). Pitching distance varies by age and division — cite USA Softball when the rule matters.
- Wrong for this sport: pitch (= soccer field), court, gridiron, rink

## Players and positions

- Pitcher, catcher, first baseman, second baseman, shortstop, third baseman, left fielder, center fielder, right fielder. (In slowpitch and some rec leagues, a 10th player called a short fielder or rover.)
- The kid at the plate is the batter or hitter.
- Wrong for this sport: striker, midfielder, attacker, lineman, libero, goalie

## Action verbs

- Pitch, throw, hit, swing, slap (slap-hitting is specific to softball — a left-handed batter starting in the box and running through contact), bunt, drag bunt, sacrifice, field, scoop, tag, slide, steal
- Pitching motion is windmill / underhand. "Throwing" describes any non-pitch throw.
- Pitches: fastball, change-up, drop ball, rise ball, screwball, curveball
- Wrong for this sport: shoot, kick, dribble, dunk, spike, body-check, tackle. Also: "overhand pitch" — softball pitching is underhand windmill in fastpitch; saying overhand misses the sport.

## Scoring

- A team scores a run when a baserunner crosses home plate.
- Hits: single, double, triple, home run
- Wrong for this sport: goal, point, basket, touchdown, kill

## Game structure

- Inning (top half, bottom half), at-bat, count (balls and strikes), three outs end the half-inning. Regulation is seven innings in most fastpitch (vs. nine in baseball, six in Little League baseball).
- Wrong for this sport: quarter, half, period, set, match

## Equipment

- Bat (softball bats are different from baseball bats — don't conflate), glove, mitt (catcher's and first baseman's), helmet with face mask (required in fastpitch), cleats (metal cleats banned in most youth softball), softball (yellow optic, larger than a baseball)
- Wrong for this sport: stick, racquet, club, puck

## Cross-sport pitfalls (most common slips)

- "Just like baseball but with a bigger ball" — write the drill in softball terms, not as a translated baseball drill. Pitching mechanics, base distances, and slap-hitting are sport-specific.
- "Overhand pitch" — wrong for fastpitch. Use "windmill" or "underhand."
- "Scored a goal" — wrong. Use "scored a run."
- "The first baseman shot the ball to second" — wrong. Threw to second.
- Mixing baseball pitch types and softball pitch types — drop ball and rise ball are softball; splitter and slider are baseball.
