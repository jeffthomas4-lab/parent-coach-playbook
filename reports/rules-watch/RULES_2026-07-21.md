## What was drafted

**`src/content/news/california-heat-safety-law-deadline-2026.md`** (draft: true)
California's SB 1248 required schools to have a written extreme-heat plan for outdoor activities — including sports practice — in place by July 1, 2026: monitoring forecasts, a named indoor alternative, staff trained to spot heat stress. The law followed the 2023 heat-related death of a 12-year-old during PE. No federal or national equivalent exists. Parent action: in California, ask your school or league to show the plan; elsewhere, this is a reasonable prompt to ask your own league coordinator whether a written heat policy exists, before the next heat wave rather than during one. Source: CalMatters (July 16, 2026), which cites and links the underlying CDE guidance and the 2024 bill text. Not covered anywhere on the site — existing heat content (`heat-index-protocols-summer-2026.md`, `summer-heat-protocol-youth-sports-2026.md`) covers temperature thresholds and guidance, not state-law mandates.
