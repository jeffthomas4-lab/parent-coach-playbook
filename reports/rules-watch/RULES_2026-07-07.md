## What was drafted

**`src/content/news/ncaa-five-year-eligibility-rule-2026.md`** (draft: true)
On June 23, 2026, the NCAA Division I Cabinet approved an age-based, five-year eligibility model: the eligibility clock now starts the academic year after high school graduation or a player's 19th birthday, whichever comes first, and runs for five years regardless of gap years or delayed enrollment. Hardship waivers and delayed-enrollment extensions are eliminated going forward. Effective for anyone enrolling full-time in college fall 2027 or later; students enrolling fall 2026 and current athletes get whichever rule works out better for them. This directly affects any family weighing a gap year, prep year, or grade reclassification for athletic reasons — the old assumption of "buy a year" no longer holds the same way. Source: NCAA.org. Not yet covered anywhere on the site, so it's a genuinely new post, not a duplicate of the existing NIL news item.
