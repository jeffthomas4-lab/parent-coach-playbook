## Lead

The final was Sunday. If your house went the way ours did, the trophy got lifted, somebody cried a little, and then Monday morning showed up with a kid in the backyard kicking a ball against the fence before anyone had made coffee.

That's the part nobody warns you about. Not the tournament. The Monday after it. A month of daily matches, favorite players, breakfast arguments about who should have started, and then it's just gone, and the kid is standing there at 7am full of something with nowhere to put it.

The instinct is to solve it with a commitment. Club team. Fall league. A camp with a deposit due Friday. The excitement is right there, practically asking to be signed up for something. We've caught ourselves reaching for the registration page more than once.

Here's the thing worth passing to the parent next to you in the bleachers: a kid's enthusiasm on the Monday after a month-long tournament is not the same as a kid's settled interest in playing more soccer. One is afterglow. The other takes weeks to show itself. On a Monday morning they look identical, and one of them costs four hundred dollars.

The good news is you don't have to decide this week. The other news is that something else is coming, and that one does have a date. Fall tryouts are about two weeks out in most places. Preseason meetings are already on calendars.

Two reads below, one for each.
