## Lead

Fall tryouts are close enough now that you can see them from here. Four weeks out, maybe five, depending on your sport and your state. The travel schedule is already posted. The last camp bag of the summer is already packed. And somewhere in a group chat this week, a parent typed the sentence that opens every August: *do you think she'll make varsity?*

We spent the past week doing something different than usual. Instead of one recruiting piece, we ran the real numbers for basically every sport we cover: baseball, softball, basketball, volleyball, soccer, football, hockey, swimming, gymnastics. The real odds of making a roster. The real odds of getting recruited past that. Nobody handed us these numbers when our own kids were coming up. We learned most of them the hard way, in a parking lot, after a cuts list had already gone up.

The short version, before you click anything: the number is smaller than you think, in every sport. That's not the sad part. The sad part is how long most families go without hearing it plainly, and how much of the summer gets spent hoping instead of just knowing.

Two things below. Plus one we're pulling back out of the archive, because tryout season is exactly when it earns its keep.
