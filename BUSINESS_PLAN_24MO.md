## 1. The bet

### What this is
A youth-sports parent media property that sits at the intersection of trust and gear-buyer intent. Editorial voice that doesn't exist anywhere else (parent-to-parent, money-honest, dry), tools that don't exist anywhere else (cost calculator, age pathways, season calendars, camps directory). Primary monetization is affiliate, secondary is display ads, third is sponsored camp listings, fourth (Year 3) is private-label gear.

### What this isn't
A general parenting blog. A recruiting site (we have a recruiting pillar, not a recruiting site). A SaaS or coaching app. A speaking platform. A book launch site. Adjacency to the Power Of Series and Chain Reaction is fine but downstream, not the design center.

### The 24-month outcome
- 100,000 monthly unique visitors by May 2028 (linear growth from 50K at month 12, +5K per month in year 2; expect step-function in practice)
- 20,000+ active email subscribers
- $4,500-6,500/month average revenue across affiliate + display ads + camp sponsorship by month 24
- Three months of validated reader data on which Path B private-label SKU to launch in Year 3
- Defensible authority through external backlinks, voice, and unique tools

### The two non-negotiables
1. **Voice integrity.** The editorial voice is the moat. Every VA-assisted gear guide, every freelance-written essay, every social post passes the Anti AI Writing checklist before publishing. If the voice slips, the trust slips, the affiliate revenue collapses, and the Year 3 product launch becomes impossible.
2. **Compatibility with the 2026 stack.** Chain Reaction manuscript and UPS football are the two things that make 2026 a success. This plan must not consume the time or attention they need. Anything in this plan that competes with those two priorities gets cut, not crammed in.

## 3. Traffic growth model

### The shape, not the line

SEO traffic does not grow linearly. It grows in steps. A piece sits at position 30-50 for 2-4 months doing nothing. Then a cluster jumps to page 1 and you get a step. Then plateau. Then another step. Plan for the plateaus or year two breaks you.

Realistic trajectory:

| Month | Total monthly visitors | What's driving it |
|---|---|---|
| 6 (Nov 2026) | 8K-12K | Schema deployment, first 8-12 ranked pieces, Facebook starts contributing |
| 12 (May 2027) | 35K-50K | First step-function from compounded SEO. November face reveal opens podcasts and press. |
| 18 (Nov 2027) | 65K-80K | Sport landing pages indexed. Pinterest contributing. First freelance writer at full speed. |
| 24 (May 2028) | 95K-115K | Maturity. Backlink portfolio compounds. Email list re-shares articles weekly. |

### The traffic mix that matters

50K visitors of essay readers and 50K visitors of gear buyers are not the same business. Same total, 5-10x revenue gap. The mix to drive toward by month 24:

- 45% gear-buyer intent (lands on a What to Buy guide or sport landing page)
- 25% decision/script intent (lands on a Decision or Script piece)
- 20% essay/voice intent (lands on a Drive There/Game/Drive Home piece)
- 10% tool intent (lands on cost calculator, age pathways, camps directory)

The gear-buyer 45% is what funds the operation. The essay 20% is what makes the audience product-launch-ready in Year 3 and what gets shared into Facebook groups. Both have to grow.

### The four traffic engines

1. **SEO.** Primary. ~70% of total traffic by month 24. Built through systematic gear guides, sport landing pages, and the existing essay backlog ranking.
2. **Email re-share.** ~10%. The Friday newsletter sends 500-1,500 click-throughs per send by month 24.
3. **Social referral.** ~12%. Facebook groups, Pinterest, Instagram Reels. Mostly Facebook in year one, Pinterest and Instagram catching up year two.
4. **Direct + earned media.** ~8%. Backlinks from governing bodies, parenting publications, and local press. Slow to build, durable once built.

### The content mix for SEO + voice + product research

40 / 30 / 20 / 10 split across every quarter:

- **10% product research.** "Is this $12 mouthguard actually fine?" pieces. Doubles as Year 3 SKU intelligence. Jeff drafts; VA pulls product specs.

### Where the next 24 months of topics come from

Five sources, ranked by quality of the resulting content:

1. **Reader email and DM responses.** Set up a one-line capture: every reader email gets one line in `READER_QUESTIONS.md` with the question and the date. By month 18 this file is the editorial calendar.
2. **Parent-coach interviews.** Five voice memos per month, 30 minutes each, with parent-coaches in Jeff's network. Each interview produces ~3 articles, ~3 scripts, ~1 decision. Audit flagged this as the highest-return research move on the table.
3. **Google Search Console queries.** Two hours per month. What are people landing on, what are they bouncing from, what are they searching for that we partially answer. Patterns rewrite the content roadmap quarterly.
4. **The CONTENT_ROADMAP backlog.** ~9-12 months of topics already mapped. Continue executing; replace persona references with single Editorial byline.
5. **Coverage matrix gaps.** Run the NEXT_AUDIT.md audit. Activity × age cells with zero reads get filled by priority of search volume and audience size.

### Voice protection rules (locked)

- No piece publishes without passing the Anti AI Writing checklist. Banned words, banned patterns, em dashes, hedging language, three-paragraph rule.

The camps directory is structurally well-positioned for monetization but no revenue layer is built. Two revenue mechanisms:

**Mechanism A: Claim listings (free tier with paid upgrade).**

- Premium claimed listing ($199-299/year): adds 3 photos, longer description, registration deep-link with conversion tracking, priority placement in their geographic region's results.

**Mechanism B: Featured placement.**
- Per-region or per-sport featured slots. Sponsored badge, prominent placement on the relevant filter view. $99-299 per slot per month depending on region size.
- Cap: maximum 1 featured slot per (region × sport) at any time. Editorial wall: featured placement is bought, not earned, and is labeled. We do not editorialize sponsored camps.

## 8. Distribution channels

Pulled from OWNERSHIP_ACTION_LIST_2026-05-05.md. Sequencing matters. Don't launch all four at once.

### Phase 1 (months 1-3): Facebook only

3 posts per week (Monday question, Wednesday excerpt, Friday voice piece). 30 min daily group engagement across 10-15 parent groups. Anonymous-friendly until the November face-reveal.

**Targets at 90 days:** 200-500 page followers, 1-2 organic article shares from group members, one weekly DM from a parent asking a real question.

### Phase 2 (months 4-6): add Instagram

Reels are the engine. 30-second clips with text overlay and quiet b-roll. Carousels work for "what to say after a game at age X" with 6-8 panels. Visual identity (Fraunces, cream, terracotta) translates beautifully to the platform.

4-5 posts per week: 2 reels, 2 carousels, 1 quote post. Daily stories. Save Highlights organized by Drive There / Game / Drive Home / Parent-Coach.

### Phase 3 (months 5-8): add Pinterest

Slow to start, compounds for years. 5-10 pins per week in one batched session. Cost calculator screenshots, packing lists, drive-home script cards. Each pin needs text-on-image because Pinterest is search-driven.

First 90 days of Pinterest produce ~zero traffic. Day 90+ is when the compounding starts.

### Phase 4 (post-November 2026): face-forward acceleration

Once Jeff's name and face go on the site, four channels open up that were locked:

1. **Podcast tour.** 1-2 per month for six months. Tudor Collegiate Strategies, NCSA-adjacent shows, sport-specific recruiting podcasts, parent-audience podcasts, parenting podcasts. Pitch: D-III head coach with two decades in the youth-to-college pipeline, telling parents what actually matters.
2. **HS coach distribution.** "I'm Jeff Thomas, head coach at Puget Sound, I wrote this guide for your parents, feel free to forward." 100 HS coaches forwarding to their parent lists is more valuable than 100K of cold search traffic.
3. **Earned media.** One Athletic / NYT Parenting / Today profile in the first six months post-reveal. Local press first (Tacoma News Tribune, Seattle Times) is easier and still moves SEO.
4. **LinkedIn presence.** Jeff personal account. Not a content treadmill, just findable and quotable when journalists or HS coaches search.

### Channels to skip
- TikTok. Voice mismatch. Possibly test via Reels cross-post in year 2.
- X / Twitter. Audience drift. Not worth the time.
- YouTube. Year 3 priority. Drills as 3-minute videos is a real channel; 533 drills × 3 min is an enormous library. Wait for production budget.
- Threads. Maybe in 2027 as parents migrate.

### Who runs distribution

Year 1: Jeff. Three hours per week.
Year 2: hire a part-time social manager at $1,500-3,000/month if affiliate revenue funds it. Or use Repurpose.io / Opus Clip plus Jeff doing captions and engagement.

## 14. Risks and how each one breaks the plan

### Risk 1: Voice slip in VA-produced or freelance content
**How it breaks:** Trust erodes, share rates drop, Google notices the AI-tells, ranking suffers, affiliate revenue collapses, Path B becomes unviable.
**Mitigation:** Anti AI Writing checklist on every piece. 30-45 min Jeff edit on every VA or writer-produced article. Quarterly audit of last 30 publishes by a fresh reader.

### Risk 2: Google update destroys gear-guide rankings
**How it breaks:** 60-80% of the affiliate revenue runs through SEO. Google's helpful-content updates have demolished thin affiliate sites. We're betting that voice + tools + author entity + first-hand experience signals protect us.
**Mitigation:** Diversify. Email list, social referral, direct backlinks. By month 18, no single channel should be more than 60% of traffic. Defensible content traits (real photos, specific anecdotes, "what to skip" sections) on every gear guide.

### Risk 3: Time conflict with Chain Reaction and UPS football
**How it breaks:** The two non-negotiable 2026 priorities. If this plan eats time they need, the plan loses.
**Mitigation:** Hard cap at 20 hrs/week through October 2026 (recruiting/season window). VA hire moved earlier if needed to compensate. Year 2 freelance writer is the buffer.

### Risk 4: Camps directory moderation overwhelm
**How it breaks:** Quality framework is solid but submissions still need human review. If volume spikes (1,000+ per month) without the trust system catching most of them automatically, Jeff loses 5+ hrs/week to moderation.
**Mitigation:** Trust-tier system (`submitters.trust_level`) auto-approves verified submitters. Quality dashboard catches aggregator domains. Hire a part-time camps moderator at $300-500/mo if it becomes the bottleneck (year 2).

### Risk 5: Affiliate program account suspension
**How it breaks:** Amazon Associates can suspend for sub-3-sales-in-180-days, FTC disclosure issues, or accidental violation of operating agreement (price display, prohibited links).
**Mitigation:** Disclosure already correct. Quarterly review of `/go/` slugs and Amazon links. Multi-network strategy means we're not single-source-of-failure on Amazon.

### Risk 6: November face-reveal complications with UPS
**How it breaks:** AD or president raises an issue with the visibility of a head coach running a public-facing media property. Worst case, a directive to take Jeff's name off.
**Mitigation:** Brief the AD before November. Frame it as institutional asset (D3 coach with public platform contributing to athletics visibility, ties to the enrollment-engine narrative). Optional disclaimer on the site that it's personal/independent of UPS. Have these conversations cheap, before the reveal, not after.

### Risk 7: Path B ambition pulls focus before Year 3
**How it breaks:** White-label work in year 1 or 2 distracts from audience building. The audience is the moat for Path B; without 100K visitors and 20K subscribers, Path B is just another startup with no distribution.
**Mitigation:** Path B is a Year 3 project. The only Path B work in the next 24 months is reader question tracking and product research articles in Q3-Q4 2027. Anything else is premature.
