# 125 Posts Plan — Round 3

The next 125 posts. Designed to round out coverage so a mom-aged-37 with two kids — one in soccer, one in dance — can find her exact moment in five clicks. Mapped to the existing eight topics so the Reads index stays clean, plus a tenth block for performing arts parents and an eleventh for summer camps, which is where revenue hits. Heavy on emotion and logistics, the two threads still under-represented. Includes a dedicated camp-safety vetting piece per request — the questions to ask about counselor training, two-deep leadership, and abuse prevention. No duplicates against current articles, drills, or sport guides.

### Revenue mechanics, in detail

**Parent-facing flow.** Mom searches "how to pick a summer baseball camp." Lands on #111. Reads the ten-minute evaluation framework. Bottom of post: "Find baseball camps in [state]" → /camps/?sport=baseball&state=WA. She filters by age, sees three options, clicks one with photos and a verified badge, registers. Camp director gets the registration. Mom comes back next May.

**Camp-director-facing flow.** Local baseball camp director searches "how do parents pick a summer camp." Reads #111 and recognizes the questions parents are now armed with. Scrolls to bottom: "Run a camp? Claim your listing for $79/year — verified badge, photos, registration link." Clicks through to claim form. We process the claim. Recurring revenue.

**Affiliate flow.** #117 (packing list) carries 8–12 /go/ links — water bottle, sunscreen, cleat bag, ice pack, etc. Each click is tracked in the affiliate sheet. #120 (forgotten gear) cross-links the labeling kit and replacement gear. We're already running /go/ infrastructure; this just feeds it more volume from camp-season search traffic.

## Voice and length notes

Every post in Jeff voice. Short paragraphs. Specific scenes. No fake-wisdom triplets, no reframe patterns, no empowerment closes. 600–900 words for most. The Hard Stuff and Rules of Play can run 900–1,200 when the moment earns it.

Each piece gets the same frontmatter pattern as existing articles. Topic + phase + age + sport (where relevant) + dek. Hero illustration tag matched to the prompt sheet so when images come back, drop-in is one line per post.

Before I write any of these, I'll cross-reference titles against the 79 existing pieces in `src/content/articles/`. If a title overlaps in spirit, I'll either kill it or sharpen the angle. Same for the 100 drills — these articles should not duplicate drill content.
