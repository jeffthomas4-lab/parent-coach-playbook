# Parent Coach Playbook Company Operating System

**Version:** 1.0
**Effective:** July 18, 2026
**Owner:** Jeff Thomas
**Operating horizon:** May 2026 through May 2028
**Review cycle:** weekly operating review, monthly business review, quarterly strategy close

## 1. Purpose and authority

This is the single human-readable operating system for Parent Coach Playbook
and Parent Coach Desk. It turns the 24-month business plan into a repeatable
company rhythm: how the company discovers demand, chooses work, protects trust,
ships, distributes, measures, maintains, learns, spends, hires, and decides
whether to continue or stop an initiative.

This document synthesizes the 24-month business plan, the ParentCoachDesk
Operating Manual, the PCD AI Operating System, the content roadmap,
monetization and marketing strategy, current technical controls, and the July
2026 build-plan audit. Those sources remain evidence and detailed references;
when they conflict on company priorities, cadence, or decision rights, this
document governs. Legal documents, security runbooks, data maps, deployment
runbooks, and editorial standards retain authority within their specialist
domains.

The operating system exists to prevent five failure modes:

1. building more product while discovery remains the binding constraint;
2. publishing volume that weakens the editorial voice;
3. mistaking code, automation, traffic, or activity for business progress;
4. allowing maintenance, legal, security, or data obligations to become
   invisible;
5. starting a new revenue line before the preceding evidence gate is clear.

## 2. The company

### 2.1 The bet

Parent Coach Playbook is a trusted youth-sports parent media property at the
intersection of honest editorial guidance and purchase intent. Parent Coach
Desk is its product and operating surface: editorial content, scripts,
decisions, tools, buying guidance, season references, and a structured camps
directory.

The moat compounds from five assets:

1. an authentic parent-coach editorial voice;
2. useful tools and structured local activity data competitors do not have;
3. privacy-minimized first-party demand signals;
4. evidence-backed relationships among sports, ages, locations,
   organizations, camps, guides, articles, products, and sources;
5. an owned email audience that is not dependent on a search or social
   algorithm.

### 2.2 What the company is not

It is not a general parenting blog, coaching SaaS, recruiting business,
speaking platform, book-launch site, automated content farm, paid-placement
directory disguised as organic search, or private-label company before the
audience earns that expansion.

### 2.3 Non-negotiables

- **Voice integrity is the moat.** Jeff retains the final voice edit on every
  material piece. AI, contractors, and agents may research and structure; they
  do not manufacture experience or recommendation reasoning.
- **Trust outranks short-term revenue.** Affiliate economics, sponsorship, and
  paid placement never determine editorial conclusions or organic directory
  ranking.
- **Chain Reaction and UPS football remain protected 2026 priorities.** Parent
  Coach Playbook operates within 15–20 founder hours per week. Work that
  competes with those priorities is cut, not crammed in.
- **Evidence precedes claims.** Code presence, a green build, a draft, or an
  agent run is not proof of a live customer or business outcome.
- **External actions require authority.** Production deployment, credentials,
  migrations, payments, publishing, outreach, provider activation, and legal
  actions remain Jeff-controlled unless a narrow written policy explicitly
  says otherwise.
- **Privacy by design.** Collect the minimum signal needed, aggregate early,
  set retention limits, support deletion, and never build a youth-sports
  surveillance asset.

## 3. Twenty-four-month outcomes and gates

The May 2028 target is:

- 100,000 monthly unique visitors;
- 20,000 active email subscribers;
- $4,500–$6,500 average monthly revenue across affiliate, display advertising,
  and camps;
- three months of validated reader evidence identifying whether a Year 3
  private-label product deserves a prototype;
- defensible authority through voice, tools, structured data, citations,
  backlinks, and direct audience relationships.

These are targets, not promises. The company advances by gates:

### Gate 1 — November 2026

Target: 8,000–12,000 monthly visitors, a working weekly email lifecycle,
initial multi-network affiliate coverage, distribution cadence, measurable
indexing improvement, and no unresolved launch-critical trust or operational
failure.

If missed: reduce production volume, diagnose discovery and indexing, preserve
voice, and do not add another channel merely to look busy.

### Gate 2 — May 2027

Target: 35,000–50,000 monthly visitors, 5,000–8,000 active subscribers,
$1,500–$3,500 monthly revenue, evidence that buying-intent pages contribute a
healthy share of traffic, and a manageable camps moderation burden.

If missed: do not hire the Year 2 writer. Continue founder plus research
support, lower the month-24 traffic target, and fix the failed acquisition or
conversion loop.

### Gate 3 — November 2027

Target: 65,000–80,000 monthly visitors, 12,000–15,000 active subscribers,
$3,000–$5,000 monthly revenue, diversified affiliate income, initial camps
revenue, and product-research evidence across multiple categories.

If missed: delay Year 3 product work and continue audience and revenue
maturation.

### Gate 4 — May 2028

Target: 95,000–115,000 monthly visitors, at least 20,000 active subscribers,
$4,500–$6,500 monthly revenue, a durable traffic mix, and three months of
product-demand evidence.

If missed: choose deliberately among continuing the media model, narrowing to
the profitable channels, or extending the evidence window. Do not launch
inventory to rescue a missed media target.

## 4. The company flywheel

The operating loop is:

**Listen → understand demand → choose the smallest evidence-backed bet → create
or improve a trusted asset → distribute it → capture minimized behavior →
measure business value → maintain what works → retire what does not → feed the
learning into the next choice.**

Every active initiative must name:

- the user problem and evidence;
- the company outcome it supports;
- the owner and time budget;
- the leading and lagging measures;
- the approval class;
- the maintenance cost;
- the next review date;
- a stop, pause, or graduation rule.

Work without those fields is a suggestion, not an operating commitment.

## 5. Discovery across every spectrum

Discovery means both how parents find the company and how the company discovers
what parents need. The company manages a portfolio, not a single algorithm.

### 5.1 Search discovery

Search remains the primary acquisition engine. The operating priorities are:

- indexable, fast, accessible pages with correct canonicals, sitemaps, robots,
  structured data, and internal links;
- authority clusters built from real coverage rather than keyword volume alone;
- a balanced traffic mix: approximately 45% buying intent, 25% decisions and
  scripts, 20% essays and voice, and 10% tools and directory intent by month 24;
- monthly Google Search Console analysis of queries, pages, indexing, position,
  click-through rate, and gaps;
- quarterly refresh, consolidation, redirect, and retirement decisions based
  on traffic, citations, overlap, freshness, and human judgment.

Technical SEO is maintained continuously, but new plumbing is not the default
answer when authority and distribution are the binding constraint.

### 5.2 AI and answer-engine discovery

The company optimizes for accurate citation and entity understanding, not
tricks aimed at one model. It maintains:

- consistent organization, site, author, article, camp, product, and source
  identities;
- clear claims with visible evidence and correction paths;
- concise answers supported by deeper first-party pages;
- structured data only where page content actually supports it;
- crawlable source and methodology pages;
- monitoring of referral traffic and cited mentions where evidence is
  available.

The company never creates fake consensus, synthetic reviews, or unsupported
first-hand experience for AI visibility.

### 5.3 Social and community discovery

Facebook groups and Pinterest are the first durable community channels because
they match the audience and reusable content. Reddit is a listening and
helpfulness channel where community rules permit brand participation.

Operating rules:

- help first; a link must be relevant and optional;
- disclose affiliation;
- stage drafts for human review;
- never automate public posting or replies without a separately approved
  channel policy;
- track first-party landing performance with governed campaign tags;
- judge channels by qualified sessions, subscribers, saves, replies, and
  assisted revenue—not follower count.

Instagram or short video enters only after an owner, repeatable production
system, and measured reason exist. A podcast and high-frequency founder-led
speaking remain off-strategy.

### 5.4 Email and direct discovery

Email is the primary owned-audience system. The required lifecycle is:

1. explicit consent;
2. confirmation or provider-supported equivalent;
3. promised resource delivery;
4. useful welcome sequence;
5. weekly Friday Letter;
6. accurate attribution and aggregate engagement measurement;
7. immediate unsubscribe and durable suppression;
8. tested delivery and failure handling.

Targets are active subscribers and meaningful engagement, not raw list size.
Email addresses never enter general analytics or content-intelligence systems.

### 5.5 Earned authority and partnerships

Backlinks and relationships are a company-level constraint. The company builds
them through governing-body references, parenting publications, local
journalism, adjacent newsletters, podcasts when appropriate, research assets,
and genuinely useful tools.

The relationship registry distinguishes a public-source candidate from an
actual relationship. No contact attempt, endorsement claim, or partnership
claim exists until a human authorizes and records it. Quarterly review measures
new referring domains, quality, relevance, resulting traffic, and durable
relationships—not email volume.

### 5.6 Local and directory discovery

The camps and activities directory compounds through:

- source-backed organization and program records;
- geographic coverage selected from parent demand and supply gaps;
- conservative deduplication and provenance;
- verification dates and visible freshness;
- provider corrections and claims behind authorization;
- neutral organic ranking based on relevance, distance, and record quality;
- clearly separated sponsored inventory if and when monetization is approved.

The database does not become parent-facing merely because records exist. Hidden
or incomplete stubs remain hidden until evidence clears the publication bar.

### 5.7 Customer and market discovery

The highest-value learning inputs are:

- reader replies and questions;
- five parent-coach interviews per month when founder capacity permits;
- onsite searches and no-result patterns after minimization;
- GSC queries and partial-answer pages;
- camp submissions, corrections, and provider questions;
- affiliate click and conversion patterns at aggregate level;
- product wear tests and comparison evidence;
- competitor and market changes that alter parent choices.

Each meaningful insight enters an evidence-backed opportunity queue with a
source date, confidence, affected audience, expected value, effort, risk,
maintenance cost, and next decision. Raw personal messages do not become a
permanent analytics lake.

## 6. Editorial and product operating system

### 6.1 Portfolio mix

Quarterly production should approximate:

- 40% buying guides and high-intent upgrades;
- 30% Drive There, Game, and Drive Home voice pieces;
- 20% tools, frameworks, decisions, and scripts;
- 10% product research that also informs potential Year 3 products.

The mix is adjusted by evidence; it is not a quota that justifies weak work.

### 6.2 Sustainable cadence

Within the founder’s Year 1 capacity, the ceiling is approximately 12–14
substantial publishes per month, including two long-form pieces per week, one
short essay per week, one monthly lead magnet, one weekly newsletter, and one
quarterly tool improvement. Existing high-volume collections are maintained
rather than expanded reflexively.

Year 2 capacity expands only after Gate 2 funds it. A research assistant may
prepare specifications, tables, FAQs, source packets, and image candidates. A
freelance writer may draft buying guides. Jeff retains recommendation logic,
voice pieces, cornerstone work, reader replies, and final approval.

### 6.3 Editorial quality gate

No material piece publishes until it passes:

- source and factual review;
- claim-risk and safety review when applicable;
- editorial voice and anti-generic-writing review;
- title, intent, and internal-link review;
- disclosure and affiliate review;
- structured-data truthfulness review;
- accessibility and image-rights review;
- explicit human editorial approval.

### 6.4 Refresh and retirement

Live content enters a deterministic 18-month review queue, with earlier review
for rules, safety, prices, affiliate destinations, recalls, and time-sensitive
facts. Refresh decisions consider traffic, citations, user demand, overlap,
commercial context, correction history, and source freshness.

No automated system deletes, redirects, consolidates, or materially rewrites a
live page. It may identify candidates and assemble evidence. A human makes the
decision and preserves redirect, correction, and audit history.

### 6.5 Parent Coach Approved

The product mark exists to make first-hand evidence legible. A product cannot
receive the mark without at least 12 weeks of use, comparison with at least two
real alternatives, wear evidence, an honest verdict, who it is wrong for, when
to skip the category, a valid human approval record, and commercial separation.

The mark is not a safety certification or universal “best.” No paid placement,
affiliate commission, manufacturer request, or automated score can award it.
The current registry is intentionally empty.

## 7. Revenue operating system

Revenue follows trust and audience evidence. The company maintains four staged
lines.

### 7.1 Affiliate

Affiliate is the first revenue line. Operate a multi-network portfolio,
maintain disclosures and link health, prohibit unsupported price and
availability claims, and measure aggregate revenue by network, destination,
page, and campaign where provider data allows.

Monthly decisions focus on which trusted pages and categories create useful
purchase outcomes. A high commission never rescues a poor recommendation.

### 7.2 Display advertising

Advertising is considered only after eligible traffic and a performance test.
Before launch, record baseline Core Web Vitals, engagement, subscriber
conversion, and user experience. After launch, enforce mobile-performance and
reader-impact thresholds. If the economics do not exceed the trust and
performance cost, reduce or remove ads.

### 7.3 Camps revenue

Free listings remain available under the quality rules. Operator claims,
premium tools, and featured placement require customer authorization,
payments, support, refunds, legal terms, data handling, and operational
readiness before activation.

Organic ranking cannot be purchased. Sponsored placement must be unmistakably
labeled and separately ranked. Paid status cannot create verification or
editorial endorsement.

### 7.4 Year 3 product option

Private-label work does not begin because a product seems interesting. It
requires Gate 4 audience evidence, at least three months of repeated product
questions and behavior, first-hand category testing, unit economics including
returns and support, counsel and insurance review, and a capped prototype.

The first prototype is a test with explicit kill thresholds, not a commitment
to become an inventory company.

### 7.5 Financial discipline

The company is founder-funded and assumes no outside capital. Every monthly
review records:

- cash collected by revenue line;
- refunds, fees, taxes payable, and direct costs;
- contractor and software expense;
- contribution margin by active line where knowable;
- cash runway and upcoming commitments;
- founder hours by workstream;
- experiments authorized, spent, and concluded.

No hire is justified by forecast revenue alone. Recurring contractor cost must
be covered by trailing, durable revenue or an explicit founder-approved
investment budget. Affiliate, advertising, sponsorship, and product income are
kept analytically separate.

## 8. Organization and decision rights

### 8.1 Human organization

Jeff is founder, editor, product owner, and final risk authority. Year 1 is
founder-led with optional research assistance after the operating templates
are stable. Year 2 adds a freelance buying-guide writer only after Gate 2.

Jeff never delegates:

- company positioning and capital allocation;
- final editorial voice approval;
- high-risk claims and public corrections;
- legal, credential, production, payment, and customer-impacting approvals;
- high-value relationships;
- product-mark awards;
- hiring and termination decisions.

Research, specifications, data preparation, routine link checks, formatting,
source packets, and approved reporting can be delegated with quality checks.

### 8.2 AI organization

AI agents are operating roles, not executives and not evidence by themselves.
The current conceptual roster covers executive coordination, editorial,
affiliate, newsletter, search/discovery, camp data, trust/support, and
intelligence functions. An agent earns an active role only when it has:

- a named human owner;
- one bounded mission and SOP;
- inputs, outputs, and data classification;
- an Analyze, Draft, Stage, or narrowly authorized Act class;
- confidence thresholds and abstention behavior;
- tests, versioning, logging, and a kill switch;
- failure escalation and a retirement rule;
- evidence that the work creates more value than review and maintenance cost.

No agent may autonomously publish, contact, purchase, delete, grant access,
move money, make a legal decision, or expand its own authority. A historic
production-write exception does not create precedent; it remains subject to
the current approval matrix and maintenance controls.

### 8.3 Approval classes

- **Analyze:** read-only report. May run unattended within approved data scope.
- **Draft:** produces a proposed artifact. Nothing is sent or published.
- **Stage:** prepares a complete change for an identified human approval.
- **Act:** performs a narrow reversible action within an explicit policy,
  threshold, logging, rollback, and kill-switch boundary. Jeff alone grants or
  expands this class.

Customer, money, legal, public, credential, production, and destructive
actions always require the applicable approval even when technically easy.

## 9. Operating cadence

### 9.1 Daily exception watch

Automated or manual checks watch only genuinely time-sensitive conditions:
privacy and deletion deadlines, security incidents, failed production health,
domain or provider failure, payment failure, broken high-value affiliate links,
and camp data jobs operating outside their policy. Normal strategy does not
become a daily interruption.

### 9.2 Weekly operating review — 30 minutes

The weekly review answers:

1. What user or business signal changed?
2. What shipped, and what outcome—not activity—did it support?
3. What broke, aged, or crossed a risk threshold?
4. Which acquisition engine gained or lost qualified discovery?
5. What did readers, parents, or providers ask for?
6. Which committed task is blocked, and by whom?
7. What should stop, pause, or be retired?
8. What are the three outcomes for the next week within founder capacity?

The weekly scorecard contains a small set of leading measures: qualified
search impressions and clicks, email net growth and engagement, content shipped
and refreshed, high-intent page performance, camp quality/freshness, affiliate
health, open trust deadlines, incidents, founder hours, and current blockers.

### 9.3 Monthly business review — 90 minutes

The monthly review reconciles:

- acquisition by channel and landing intent;
- subscriber cohort growth, engagement, and suppression health;
- revenue, direct cost, contribution, and cash commitments;
- top and declining content, refresh decisions, and coverage opportunities;
- camp supply, demand, freshness, corrections, and moderation load;
- backlinks, relationships, and distribution experiments;
- customer questions and product/category signals;
- reliability, security, privacy, accessibility, and maintenance debt;
- founder capacity and delegated work quality;
- experiment decisions: continue, change, scale, pause, or kill.

Every monthly review ends with at most three company priorities and a named
capacity allocation. New work must displace something.

### 9.4 Quarterly strategy close — four hours

The quarterly close evaluates the applicable milestone gate, P&L scenario,
traffic and audience mix, brand and voice quality, channel portfolio, content
portfolio, risk register, vendor and legal calendar, staffing trigger,
automation value, and next-quarter capacity.

Each initiative and scheduled process receives one decision: continue, improve,
graduate, pause, or retire. Zombie work is not carried forward because it once
seemed promising.

### 9.5 Annual governance review

At least annually, review entity structure, tax and accounting process,
insurance, contracts, privacy notices, terms, accessibility, intellectual
property, data inventory, vendor access, incident readiness, succession and
business continuity, and whether the company’s actual activities still match
its public representations. Qualified professionals provide legal, tax,
insurance, and accessibility conclusions; software artifacts do not.

## 10. Company scorecard

The scorecard separates leading indicators, lagging outcomes, health controls,
and gates. Unknown values remain `pending`; zero is used only when measured.

### Discovery and audience

- monthly unique visitors and sessions;
- qualified organic clicks and impressions;
- indexed public pages and material indexing exclusions;
- traffic mix by buying, decision/script, essay, tool, and directory intent;
- referring domains gained and lost;
- social/community qualified sessions and assisted subscriptions;
- direct traffic and returning-reader share;
- active subscribers, net growth, confirmation, engagement, unsubscribe, and
  suppression health.

### Trust and product quality

- editorial publishes and refreshes that passed human review;
- voice-audit pass rate;
- corrections opened, resolved, and actually applied;
- high-risk content overdue for review;
- camp records current, due, unavailable, duplicate-candidate, and source-backed;
- Parent Coach Approved products with complete evidence;
- accessibility, broken-link, structured-data, and performance exceptions.

### Revenue and economics

- revenue and contribution by affiliate, advertising, camps, and product;
- affiliate revenue per qualified high-intent session;
- camp claimed/premium retention and support burden once activated;
- email-attributed and assisted revenue where consented measurement permits;
- operating expense, contractor expense, cash runway, and founder hours;
- experiment spend, result, and decision.

### Operations and risk

- release, backup, restore, and rollback evidence health;
- open incidents and time to recovery;
- privacy and deletion deadlines;
- secrets, access, and dependency exceptions;
- scheduled processes healthy, silent, paused, or retired;
- unresolved legal, insurance, accessibility, accounting, or vendor gates;
- founder capacity variance and protected-priority conflicts.

Metric definitions must record source, owner, cadence, freshness, target,
warning threshold, stop threshold, and known limitations. A dashboard without
those fields is decoration.

## 11. Maintenance and resilience

### 11.1 Maintenance classes

- **Always-on obligations:** privacy and deletion clocks, security incidents,
  customer-impacting failures, domain/provider continuity, backups, and legal
  holds.
- **Weekly health:** high-value links, indexing regressions, camp freshness,
  correction queues, email failures, and scheduled-process silence.
- **Monthly health:** revenue reconciliation, access review, dependency and
  cost review, content winners/decliners, relationship state, and data quality.
- **Quarterly health:** content freshness, disaster recovery evidence,
  accessibility sampling, risk register, vendor fitness, automation ROI, and
  process retirement.
- **Annual health:** legal, tax, insurance, contracts, privacy, accessibility,
  intellectual property, continuity, and strategic fit.

### 11.2 Seasonal maintenance mode

From August through November, Parent Coach Playbook yields founder attention to
UPS football and Chain Reaction. Build work pauses or becomes report-only.
Automated writes are disabled unless a separate year-round obligation and
authorization exists. The company continues only the always-on obligations and
urgent continuity checks. December begins with a quarterly close, not an
automatic restart of every paused task.

### 11.3 Reliability standard

Production changes use one protected deployment authority, immutable artifacts,
same-SHA staging evidence, smoke and exact-asset checks, secret-name
preservation, rollback targets, and sanitized receipts. Backups are valuable
only when independent upload, retrieval, and restore are proven over time.

No production green status is inferred from local tests. No incident is closed
without cause, user impact, correction, prevention, owner, and verification.

## 12. Risk system

The standing company risks are:

1. voice degradation;
2. search-platform concentration;
3. founder-capacity conflict;
4. camps moderation and data-quality overload;
5. affiliate suspension or concentration;
6. premature monetization weakening trust;
7. premature Year 3 product work;
8. privacy, child-safety, legal, accessibility, or insurance failure;
9. production, credential, backup, or vendor failure;
10. automation acting beyond evidence or authority;
11. stale content or relationships being represented as current;
12. revenue forecasts driving recurring costs before cash exists.

Every risk has an owner, leading indicator, mitigation, contingency, review
cadence, and trigger. A high risk cannot be accepted silently; Jeff records the
decision and expiry date. Legal and safety questions escalate to qualified
professionals rather than being “resolved” by an agent.

## 13. Experiment system

Every growth or monetization experiment starts with:

- hypothesis;
- target audience and problem;
- channel and artifact;
- baseline;
- primary success measure and guardrails;
- maximum money and founder hours;
- start and end dates;
- consent and data rules;
- minimum sample or explicit qualitative standard;
- scale, iterate, and kill thresholds;
- owner and approver.

Examples include a paid lead-magnet acquisition test, a community-distribution
cadence, a newsletter cross-promotion, a new high-intent authority cluster, or
a regional camps outreach pilot. Public posting, ad spend, outreach, and
provider changes require separate approval even when the experiment design is
complete.

Results are retained whether positive or negative. A failed experiment that
prevents repeated waste is a useful company asset.

pending. Establish channel traffic, intent mix, indexing, active email,

2. weekly Friday Letter with complete provider evidence;

three outcomes, explicitly records deferred work, and monitors conflict with
Chain Reaction and UPS football. Seasonal maintenance mode remains binding.

## 16. How this operating system is maintained

The master document changes only through a dated review that identifies the
source evidence, decision owner, affected cadence or gate, and superseded rule.
Machine-readable scorecards and registers may evolve underneath it, but they
cannot silently expand authority or redefine success.

At every quarterly close:

1. update actuals and evidence status;
2. decide each initiative and scheduled process;
3. revise the next quarter’s capacity and targets;
4. record changed assumptions;
5. verify public claims still match operations;
6. archive superseded execution plans while preserving decision history;
7. publish one concise internal handoff naming what is complete, pending,
   blocked, stopped, and next.

The company wins by learning faster without losing its voice, trust, or focus.
The operating system exists to make that discipline routine.
