## The reusable angle template

Five templates already work, proven by what's live today for football, basketball, baseball, hockey, and soccer, the best-covered sports at 15+:

1. **"{Sport} recruiting: what parents need to know."** The general-orientation piece. Already live for baseball, basketball, football, soccer, hockey (as "recruiting guide"). Missing for the other 21.

2. **"Varsity {sport} [tryouts / roster]: the real odds."** How making the team actually works at the high school level, playing time math, the thing nobody tells freshman parents. Live for basketball and football. This is the highest-value template to replicate, it answers the question every 14-to-15-year-old's parent is actually Googling in August.

3. **"When your kid doesn't get recruited for {sport}."** The honest 93%-don't-play-in-college piece, specific to the sport. Live for football. This is a retention and trust piece as much as an SEO piece, it's the payoff of the site's whole voice.

4. **A sport-specific structural quirk at the varsity level.** This one doesn't templatize word for word, it has to be true to the sport: baseball got "pitch counts and arm care at varsity," soccer got "club vs. high school, the conflict nobody resolves for you," hockey got "the juniors math." Every sport has one of these, the specific 15+ tension a parent hits and has never seen explained plainly. Requires knowing the sport, not just filling a template.

5. **A recruiting-adjacent tool or process piece.** Soccer's "college ID camp guide" is the model, a specific, actionable thing a family can do (camps, combines, film, contacting coaches) rather than a general orientation.

### The arts variant (band, choir, theater, dance, ballet)

"Recruiting" isn't the right word, but the same five slots map cleanly:

1. College audition process for the discipline (BFA/BM programs, conservatory auditions, what a pre-screen actually is).
2. "Making the top ensemble as a senior: the real odds" (drum major, all-state choir, lead roles, company placement).
3. "When your kid doesn't get into the program they wanted." Same honesty, different stakes.
4. The discipline's own structural quirk (marching band's college-scholarship-via-band-not-music angle, theater's "is community theater or school theater the better credential," ballet's pre-professional-placement crunch, already partially covered in `ballet-pre-professional-placement.md` but at 13-14, not 15+).
5. A tool or process piece (all-state audition prep checklist, portfolio/reel building for a BFA application).

## Sequencing recommendation

Don't go sport by sport in roster order. Three tiers:

**Tier 1, do first (13 sports, zero articles today):** flag-football, football-7v7, lacrosse-boys, lacrosse-girls, cross-country, crew, martial-arts, cheer, stunt, band, choir, dance, ballet. Prioritize the higher-participation ones within this tier first: flag-football, lacrosse-boys, lacrosse-girls, cross-country, cheer, band, dance before the smaller-audience ones (crew, martial-arts, stunt). Note again that flag-football and football-7v7 and lacrosse-boys need a whole-sport content look, not just a 15+ pass.

**Tier 2 (13 sports, thin coverage):** top each up from its current 1-3 count to 5, using whichever of the five templates it's missing.

**Tier 3, ongoing:** once every sport hits 5, this becomes a normal content-maintenance line item, not a special project.

## Batch sizing

Same lesson as this session's Task 3: don't attempt 96 articles in one sitting. A batch of 20-25 (roughly one tier-1 sub-group) run through the same anti-AI-writing-guide check and internal-linking pass (each new 15+ article should immediately get its in-prose link to that sport's gear guide and pathway, the way the 165 articles from this session did, not left to a future linking sprint) is a realistic single-session unit. At that size, this is a 4 to 5 session project, not a one-shot.

## What this doesn't cover

This scopes article volume only. It doesn't touch whether the pathway pages' existing 15+ bands (already present for all 26, unlike the articles) need deepening, and it doesn't touch the guide pages' 15+ sections, which are consistently the thinnest part of every guide (prose only, no product cards, as seen when `football.md`'s missing 15+ section was rebuilt earlier this session). Worth a follow-up scope of its own if the article gap gets closed first.
