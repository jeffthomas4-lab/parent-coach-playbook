## Short version (recommended for the application form)

The Parent-Coach Playbook (parentcoachplaybook.com) is an editorial site for parents who coach, drive, organize, and supervise their kids' youth sports and activities. Visitors can read short field notes and longer essays from three contributors on topics like coaching your own kid, the conversation in the car after a game, pre-game routines, gear that lasts more than one season, and what to say to a kid who didn't make varsity. They can subscribe to a weekly email newsletter, download a free 12-page PDF guide called The Drive Home Playbook, and browse curated gear recommendations organized by sport and age band.

The audience is parents and parent-coaches of kids ages 5 through 15+, primarily moms, in eight youth sports (baseball, softball, soccer, basketball, football, hockey, lacrosse, volleyball), plus parents whose kids are in theater, band, choir, or dance. Roughly 60 percent of readers are mothers, 40 percent fathers, with most readers between 30 and 50 years old.

We will promote youth sports equipment and parenting-adjacent products. Primary categories include: youth gloves, bats, balls, cleats, shin guards, helmets, mouth guards, and other protective gear by sport; team and practice essentials such as water bottles, cooler bags, sideline chairs, and cooler-pack rotations; organization tools like family planners, magnetic calendars, and team-mom-bag essentials; and books on parenting, coaching, and youth development. Every recommendation will be a product that one of our contributors has personally used for at least a season. Affiliate links route through clean redirect URLs (parentcoachplaybook.com/go/[product]) and are clearly marked on the site.

## If they ask for more detail (a longer expanded version)

The Parent-Coach Playbook is an editorial publication serving parents who are deeply involved in their children's youth sports and other activities. The site contains weekly short-form posts (200 to 500 words) and occasional longer essays (800+ words) from three contributors. Maren Bell writes most posts as the lead voice, focused on the day-to-day experience of being a sports parent. Dan Kowalski writes from the dad-coach perspective, particularly on coaching his own children. Jeff Thomas is the founder and editorial director, a college football head coach at the University of Puget Sound, and writes the longer cornerstone pieces.

Content is organized around a three-part framework called The Three Drives: pre-game preparation (the drive to practice or games), in-game coaching execution, and the post-game conversation (the drive home). Content is tagged by sport and by age band, so a parent can filter to articles relevant to their 9-year-old soccer player or their 14-year-old volleyball player.

Visitors can: read articles, sort articles by sport and age, subscribe to the weekly email newsletter, download free PDF guides, browse a curated gear hub, and contact the editors with story leads or questions.

The site does not host user-generated content, comments, or any form of social interaction beyond email newsletter sign-ups and editorial contact.

Products we will promote are limited to youth sports gear, family logistics products (planners, bags, water bottles), and books on parenting and coaching. We do not promote supplements, alcohol, gambling, or anything outside the youth sports and parenting category.

Traffic sources will be: organic search (long-tail queries like "what to say to my kid after a bad game"), referrals from coaching networks and youth sports leagues, an email newsletter delivered weekly, and word of mouth among parent-coaches.
