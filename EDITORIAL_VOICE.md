# Editorial voice

Replaces VOICES.md. The site has moved to a single editorial voice. Every piece is written and edited by the **Parent Coach Playbook Editorial**.

This file is the rules for that voice. Anyone writing for the site, including humans and AI assistants, follows what's here.

---

## Who the editorial is

Plural. Implied team. The byline carries weight because it represents collective experience across many sports, ages, and parent-coach years. We are not a single person and we don't pretend to be.

Reader's mental model of who we are: a small group of parents who have done youth sports across many seasons, who have coached at several levels, and who care about being useful more than being clever.

---

## Voice rules

1. **Plain language.** The advice first. The reason second. The example third.
2. **Short paragraphs.** Three sentences max.
3. **No em dashes.** Period or colon. We mean it.
4. **No banned words.** The list lives in `ABOUT ME/Anti AI Writing.txt` (delve, leverage, robust, seamless, pivotal, navigate, embark, journey, unlock, unleash, empower, etc.).
5. **No "not just X, but Y"** framings. Rewrite straight.
6. **No fake-wisdom triplets.** Three vague phrases stacked to sound profound is a tell. Cut to one or rewrite.
7. **No empowerment closes.** Endings that wrap with "and that, ultimately, is the gift of youth sports" go in the trash. End on a fact or an action.
8. **First person.** "We" is fine. "I" referring to a single named person is not.
9. **Second person.** "You" is encouraged. Talking to the reader like they are in the room with us is the whole point.
10. **Specifics.** Real numbers, real cleat sizes, real ages, real distances. Not "many," not "often," not "some."

---

## Pacing

- A short read (`format: note`) is 200-500 words.
- A longer read (`format: essay`) is 600-1500 words.
- A drill or coaching tip is 200-600 words.
- A Buy guide is 400-800 words.

---

## Structure

- Lead with the point.
- Subheads are short and load-bearing. The reader scanning subheads should see the structure of the argument.
- Use the title-italics convention: wrap the conceptual hinge of the headline in `*asterisks*` so it renders italic.
- One italic phrase per H1.

---

## What we don't do

- We don't tell parents what to feel.
- We don't give medical advice.
- We don't predict college recruiting outcomes.
- We don't shame any choice a family makes about youth sports unless that choice involves harming a kid.
- We don't write content for SEO. We write for the parent reading. Search comes second.

---

## What we do

- We cite governing bodies when rules are involved (USA Baseball, USA Softball, US Lacrosse, USA Hockey, USA Swimming, US Soccer, NFHS, NOCSAE).
- We link to the National Organizations directory for background.
- We update pieces when facts change. Corrections live at the bottom of the post with a date.
- We answer reader email at parentcoachplaybook@gmail.com.

---

## Examples

**Good:** "The shortstop cone is in the slot between second and third. Place it there. The kid stands there. They learn the position."

**Bad:** "Coaches should leverage cones as a dynamic tool to empower their young athletes to discover their positional identity through repeated, intentional practice."

**Good:** "Tee-ball doesn't have strikeouts for a reason. The rules were written by people who knew that a six-year-old who strikes out three times in a row won't come back next week."

**Bad:** "Tee-ball, in its essence, is more than a game. It is a journey of discovery, where young hearts learn to navigate the complex tapestry of competition."

The first option in each pair is the voice. The second is what we delete.
