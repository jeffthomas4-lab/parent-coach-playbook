## Lead

Friday Letter No. 1. Some of you signed up months ago and heard nothing until today. That's on us, and the fix starts with this letter, every Friday, one short read.

First, the thing you were promised. What to Say When is a 28-page field guide: twenty-three scripts for the moments before and after the game, organized by age, calibrated for the car and the kitchen.

**[Download What to Say When (PDF) →](https://parentcoachdesk.com/what-to-say-when.pdf)**

Direct link. No hoops. Every other free download we've shipped, including the practice plan template, lives at [parentcoachdesk.com/welcome](https://parentcoachdesk.com/welcome/).

Now the week. The World Cup final is Sunday, and if your house is like most houses we know, a kid who plays baseball or volleyball or nothing at all has spent a month talking about players they'd never heard of in May. Enjoy it while it lasts.

And quietly, behind the soccer noise: fall tryouts are about four weeks out. Cuts lists go up in August. Two of the three reads below are for the final. The other one, plus the piece we're pulling back out of the archive, is for the clock.
