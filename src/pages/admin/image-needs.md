## Brand consistency (paste this with every prompt)

> **Style:** Editorial illustration, not photorealistic. Soft, painterly,
> slightly textured. Quiet domestic moments and sideline scenes — never
> action shots or stock-photo-style athletes mid-game. Composition reads
> calm and human. Backgrounds always favor depth and natural light over
> graphic flatness.
>
> **Palette:** Cream paper (#FAF6EE) and warm paper (#F2E8DD) backgrounds.
> Ink (#2D2520) for grounding. Terracotta (#BC8F84) and honey (#D4AB6A)
> as warm accents. Sage green (#8FA68C) for outdoors. Bone (#E7DECB) and
> linen (#DDD2BD) as neutrals. No saturated reds, blues, or neons.
>
> **Format:** WebP, 1600×1000 for hero images, 1200×630 for OG cards,
> 1400×600 for landscape dividers. Transparent only for icons; backgrounds
> always paper-warm or scene.
>
> **Avoid:** stock-photo gloss, smiling-stranger faces, logos, branded
> equipment, sponsorships, perfect lighting, motion blur, anything that
> reads as advertising. Faces obscured or backlit when possible.

## File naming convention

- All lowercase, kebab-case
- Format: `.webp` for hero/inline imagery (better compression), `.png` only for icons/logos
- Hero images: 1600×1000 (article hero ratio)
- OG cards: 1200×630
- Section dividers: 1400×400
- Sport landing heroes: 1600×1000

## Where to drop them

All into `public/illustrations/` to match existing convention. Reference in markdown frontmatter as `hero: /illustrations/your-file.webp` and in Astro page templates as `<img src="/illustrations/your-file.webp" alt="..." />`.

## Alt text reminder

Every image needs descriptive alt text per your accessibility statement (WCAG 2.2 AA). When you add a hero to an article's frontmatter, also add `heroAlt: "..."`. The site's ArticleLayout already supports the `heroAlt` field.
