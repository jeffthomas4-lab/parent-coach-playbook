headline: "Summer heat protocols: what most state associations require before a coach can pull kids off the field"
summary: "As outdoor seasons ramp up, it is worth knowing the trigger. Most state high school associations and youth leagues follow the Korey Stringer Institute's recommendations: when heat index hits 104°F, all outdoor activity stops. Practices between 95°F and 104°F heat index must include mandatory rest breaks every 30 minutes. Many youth rec leagues have adopted these same thresholds but are not required to. If your program does not have a written heat policy posted, ask the coordinator in writing. You want the answer before July."
