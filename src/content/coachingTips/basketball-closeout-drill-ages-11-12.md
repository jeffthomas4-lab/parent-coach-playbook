A closeout is a sprint to a shooter followed by a controlled stance to contest without fouling. This drill teaches the sprint and the transition into defensive stance.

**Equipment needed:** One basketball, cones to mark three-point spots, 6 kids (3 shooters in rotation, 3 defenders in rotation).

**Setup:** Mark three three-point spots on the court (left wing, right wing, top). Shooters stand at these spots with the ball. Defenders start in the paint, 30 feet away.

**How to run it:**

1. Coach points to a shooter. Defender sprints toward them.
2. Defender must sprint hard, then plant and transition into defensive stance within 2-3 feet of the shooter (no fouling).
3. Shooter passes back to coach or makes a shot attempt (defender contests but doesn't block).
4. Defender resets and goes to the next spot as directed.
5. Do 15 closeouts per defender.

**What to look for:** Sprint effort and stance transition. The sprint should be hard, then a controlled plant to avoid fouling
