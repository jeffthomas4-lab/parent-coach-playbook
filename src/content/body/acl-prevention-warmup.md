The single highest-ROI body intervention in youth sports is a structured warm-up before practice and games. Programs like FIFA 11+ (soccer) and PEP (basketball, lacrosse, all field sports) have been shown in multiple studies to cut ACL injury rates by 30-50% in adolescent female athletes.

The biology is well-established. Female athletes between 12 and 18 are 4-8 times more likely than male athletes to tear an ACL in cutting and pivoting sports. The reasons are anatomical and neuromuscular. Wider Q-angles. Quad-dominant landing patterns. Less hamstring co-contraction. The warm-up programs target each of those.

**What the warm-up actually is.** A structured 10-15 minute sequence that hits jogging patterns, dynamic stretches, strength activation (glutes, hamstrings, calves), single-leg balance, plyometrics, and proper landing mechanics. FIFA 11+ has three parts: running, strength/balance/plyometrics, and running again. PEP runs similar.

**Why most teams don't do it.** It takes time. Coaches feel pressure to start drills. Parents don't push for it. The warm-up adds 10-15 minutes that some coaches feel they can't afford. The science says they can't afford to skip it.

**What to ask your team's coach.** Are we doing a structured warm-up before every practice and game? If yes, which one? If no, what would it take to add one? At the club level, this is usually a coachable ask. At the school level, the athletic trainer is often the right contact.

**The kids who skip the warm-up.** They are at higher injury risk. The kid who shows up late and runs straight into a scrimmage is the kid in your nightmare scenario. Make this a non-negotiable in your house.

**For older kids who've already had a knee injury.** The warm-up is more important, not less. Coordination with a sports physical therapist is the next layer. Most ACL-reconstruction return-to-play protocols include a permanent prehab routine. Don't drop it once "cleared."

The warm-up is free. The injury isn't. ACL reconstruction surgery for an adolescent runs $20,000-50,000, requires 9-12 months of rehab, and increases the risk of a re-tear or second knee injury significantly. Eleven minutes before practice. Take them.
