Your team has a group chat with 24 parents in it. One parent posts six times a day. Most of them are critiques of the coach, the ref, the field, the schedule. The chat went from useful to unbearable in three weeks.

**Why it spreads**

Vent posts get likes. Those likes pull more vent posts. The chat normalizes the venting and parents who wouldn't have complained on their own start chiming in.

**What you owe the team**

You don't owe a public correction. The vent parent will not be reformed in front of an audience. You will be the second-most-annoying parent in the chat by Thursday.

**What works**

Mute the chat. Not leave. Mute. You stay in the chat for the logistical info. You stop seeing the venting in real time.

Two of the better parents agree to handle logistics privately and post only the must-know info. Game time changes. Field changes. Snack reminders. The chat becomes a low-volume utility, not a vent space.

**The harder version**

If the venting starts being directed at the coach by name, or starts targeting a specific kid, or starts being racial, that is a different problem. Someone has to address it. The team parent or the manager. Not the coach. Not the kid's parent.

The script is one sentence. *Hey, the chat is getting heavy. Can we keep it to logistics? Anything else, take it offline.*

Most vent parents stop when one parent says that. The few who don't, you mute permanently and stop reading.

**Your kid sees the chat**

If your kid has access to your phone, your kid sees the chat. The kid reads it. The kid hears their teammates' parents complaining about the coach the kid is supposed to trust.

This poisons the team in a way the parents don't see. Your kid stops trusting their coach because the parents on the chat aren't trusting the coach.

That's the cost of the vent chat. The coach loses the room before the season starts.

**The team chat that works**

Logistics only. One person posts. Replies are reactions, not new posts. Anything else, take it private. The chat becomes useful again, and the team rebuilds trust in the coach.

That's the bar. Don't aim higher.
