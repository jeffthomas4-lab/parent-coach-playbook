Middle school basketball tryouts are, for most kids, the first time a roster has ever told them no. Twelve to fifteen spots, sixty kids in the gym, three days. Here's what's actually being evaluated, and what your job is during the week everyone's stomach hurts.

**Coaches are watching everything except the scoreboard.** Every school coach can teach a shooting form. What they can't teach in a season: sprinting back on defense every single time, listening during the huddle instead of practicing a crossover, boxing out without being reminded, the pass to the open kid instead of the contested hero shot. In a three-day tryout, effort and coachability are the only stats with a real sample size, and veteran coaches pick accordingly. The kid who dives for one loose ball gets remembered longer than the kid who hit three threes.

**The AAU question, honestly.** Club experience shows up at tryouts as conditioning, game speed, and comfort with contact, and that's a real edge. What it is not is a reservation. Every November, gyms across the country cut kids whose parents assumed the club fee bought a school roster spot, and keep rec kids who defended and hustled. School coaches, who watch their own hallways all year, are notably immune to travel resumes. We mapped [the whole rec-club-AAU ladder](/drive-there/basketball-levels-rec-school-club-aau/) and where school ball fits in it.

**The week of, your job is logistics and calm.** Sleep, real breakfasts, shoes that fit, and a ride that shows up early. The [night-before-tryouts script](/drive-there/night-before-tryouts/) covers the conversation; the short version is lower the stakes, name your unconditional column, and skip the scouting report. Kids tryout tight when they're carrying their parent's hopes along with their own.

**If the list has their name on it:** celebrate that night, then say the quiet thing once: the roster is earned again every practice. Freshman-team minutes beat varsity bench at this age, every time, and a coach who plays your kid 25 minutes a game on the B team is developing them faster than a varsity sweatsuit ever will.

**If the cut comes:** the [24-hour rule](/drive-home/after-the-cut-the-24-hour-rule/) governs. No fixing, no coach emails, no silver linings the first night, just food and presence. The path back is real and coaches respect it: rec league or club season for game reps, a driveway habit, maybe a manager role if they want to stay close to the program, and a body that shows up next November in shape. Some of the best high school players in every town got cut in 7th grade; almost none of their parents emailed the coach about it.

**The bigger truth to hold onto:** school tryouts measure one week of one year of a long athletic life. The kid is not a roster decision, and the ride home, win or lose, is where they learn whether you know that. The [scripts library](/scripts/) has the words for every version of that drive.
