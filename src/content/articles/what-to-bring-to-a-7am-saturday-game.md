The schedule says 7am. You wake up at 5:45. You are not ready. Your kid is not ready. Here is what you need.

**Coffee for you**

A travel mug. Filled before you leave. The field has no good coffee. The drive is your one good drink window.

**Breakfast for the kid**

A banana. A piece of toast with peanut butter. Eaten in the car. Don't try a sit-down breakfast at 6am. Won't work.

**Real food for after**

A small cooler with the post-game food. Cheese stick. More fruit. Crackers. The kid will be hungrier after the game than they were before.

**The kid's gear, double-checked**

Jersey, shorts, socks, shin guards, cleats. Lay it out the night before. Verify in the car at the field, not at home. Forgotten shin guards are common.

**A blanket**

For the bench. For the bleachers. For the back of the car if the warm-up went long. 7am is colder than 7pm.

**Hand warmers**

Not for the kid. For you. Two of them. One in each pocket. They make the difference between watching the game and shivering through it.

**A folding chair**

If the field has no bleachers. Most early-morning fields don't.

**A real jacket**

Not your kid's jacket. Your jacket. You will be standing for 90 minutes in 40 degrees. Wear the warmer one.

**Water**

For the kid, two bottles. For you, one.

**A change of socks for the kid**

The wet grass at 7am is wet. By 8am, the kid's first pair is soaked. The second pair makes the post-game car ride civilized.

**Hand sanitizer**

The portable bathrooms at most fields are bad. Hand sanitizer goes a long way.

**Wipes**

For the inevitable mud, snot, drool, bird poop, juice spill. One pack covers most.

**A trash bag**

For the wet stuff to go home in. The trunk thanks you.

**A small tip you'll forget**

Sunscreen even at 7am. The sun comes up by 7:45. The kid is out for two hours. Sunscreen on once before you leave.

**The thing not to bring**

Toys for the bleachers. The kid does not need a tablet at the field. The game is the entertainment.

**The 5:45 ritual**

Bag is packed Friday night. Coffee starts brewing automatically at 5:50. Kid's clothes are in a pile by their bed.

You can be at the field by 6:50 if you don't add steps.

The 7am Saturday game is survivable. With the right list, it can even be pleasant.

Drive carefully. Most morning drivers are still half asleep too.
