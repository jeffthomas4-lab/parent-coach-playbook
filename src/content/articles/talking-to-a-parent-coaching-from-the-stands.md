At 5-7, parents are louder than the kids. A parent stands up and yells "Keep your feet moving!" or "Talk to each other!" The kid hears it. So does every other kid. So do you.

Catch this parent after the game, not during halftime. Private conversation only.

Say: "I noticed you calling coaching cues from the stands on Saturday. I know you're trying to help. Here's what happens: your kid listens to you instead of me, and the other kids look at their parents instead of the field. For this age, I need them focused in one direction."

Then: "If you want to help your kid improve, we can give you three things to work on at home. That's where the coaching happens."

Most parents stop immediately. They didn't realize they were doing it. They thought the team was theirs to develop, not just their child. At this age, that's an honest mistake.

A few won't listen. Then you're friendly but firm: "I need you to watch from the bleachers like everyone else. That's the rule."

Don't shame them. Don't make it personal. They're invested in their kid's success. That's not a bad thing. It's just misdirected. You're not asking them to care less. You're asking them to care in a way that actually helps.
