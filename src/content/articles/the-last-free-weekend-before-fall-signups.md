Registration opens Monday at 9am. We know that because the league sent three reminder emails this week, and because our kid asked on Wednesday whether we'd have the form ready to submit the second the portal goes live, since spots for the popular time slots fill within the first hour most years. That means this weekend, the one sitting right before the portal opens, is the last stretch of days where nothing on the family calendar depends on anyone else's schedule. We noticed that on Thursday and decided on purpose not to let it slide past us the way it usually does.

Most years this weekend just happens. It's the one between the last week of camp and the first week back-to-school shopping, and it gets filled with errands and laundry and whatever chores got pushed off all summer, without anyone naming it as anything in particular. This year we named it. Not with a big production, just a conversation at dinner about what we actually wanted the two days to hold, given that they were the last two days before practices and games and carpools started dictating the shape of every evening again.

**We picked one thing that had nothing to do with sports at all.** Saturday morning we drove forty minutes to a lake none of us had been to before, packed a lunch, and stayed until the kids were sunburned and tired in the good way. It wasn't a special occasion by any normal measure. But it was the kind of unstructured, undirected day that gets rare fast once fall registration locks in three practices a week and two weekend games, and doing it deliberately, as the last one of its kind for a while, made it feel different than an ordinary Saturday would have.

**We also used part of the weekend to actually look at the calendar together, out loud, before it filled in.** Our kids are old enough now to have opinions about which time slots work and which don't, and Sunday afternoon we sat with the draft schedule the league had posted and talked through it as a family instead of just submitting whatever slot opened first on Monday morning. That conversation wouldn't have happened if we'd waited until the portal was live and everyone was scrambling to click submit before a slot filled. Doing it a day early, calmly, meant the actual registration moment on Monday was just data entry, not a decision.

**Part of the weekend went to noticing what worked last season and what didn't.** Over dinner Saturday we asked our kids what they wanted more of this fall and what they wanted less of, genuinely open questions, not leading ones. One kid said they wanted fewer nights with two things scheduled back to back. Another said they actually liked being busy and wanted to add something new. Neither answer was right or wrong. Both were useful information we had time to act on because we asked before the forms were already submitted, instead of after.

**We didn't try to cram in every last-summer thing on some imagined bucket list.** There's a version of this weekend that turns into a frantic checklist, one last trip to every place, one final round of every summer activity, treated like a funeral for the season instead of a transition into the next one. We skipped that version on purpose. The lake trip was enough. Padding the weekend with obligations would have defeated the point of protecting two days that didn't have any.

**By Sunday night, the calendar had already started filling back in anyway.** A text from a teammate's parent about carpool. An email confirming a practice field assignment. The portal wasn't even open yet and the season was already leaking into the weekend at the edges, the way it always does. That's fine. We weren't trying to hold the season off forever. We were just trying to notice the specific two days before it arrived, instead of discovering afterward that we'd let them go by unremarked, the way we usually do.

Monday at 9am we'll be sitting at the kitchen table with the form ready, submitting within the first few minutes the way the popular slots require. The season will start being the season again, full of scheduled things and other people's timelines. We got one weekend that belonged only to us first. That's not nothing, even if it's not much.
