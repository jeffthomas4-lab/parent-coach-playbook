# Affiliate networks to apply for

Sorted by priority. Apply to tier 1 first, tier 2 next, tier 3 only if you have bandwidth. Each has a one-line note on what they're for and a typical commission range. After approval, send the agent the credentials and tracking IDs and it will wire them through `/go/`.

---

## Tier 1 — apply this week

These cover 80% of what your audience buys. All are free to apply, take 1–7 days for approval.

### 1. Amazon Associates
**Apply at:** https://affiliate-program.amazon.com
**Covers:** Almost everything. Cleats, gloves, water bottles, foam rollers, books, kitchen stuff, the long tail.
**Commission:** 1–10% depending on category. Sports gear is roughly 3%.
**Approval:** Fast. Usually 24 hours. You need 3 qualifying sales in 180 days to stay in.
**What we need from you after approval:** Your associate tag (looks like `parentcoach-20`).

### 2. Dick's Sporting Goods
**Apply at:** https://www.dickssportinggoods.com/s/affiliate-program (also runs through Impact Radius)
**Covers:** Mid-range cleats, helmets, bats, mitts, mouthguards, athletic apparel.
**Commission:** 5–8%. Higher than Amazon for sport-specific gear.
**Approval:** 5–10 days. They review your site for fit.
**What we need from you after approval:** Your Impact Radius tracking link or partner ID.

### 3. ShareASale
**Apply at:** https://www.shareasale.com/info/affiliates
**Covers:** A network that hosts hundreds of niche programs. Once you're in, you can apply to specific merchants on their platform — many youth-sports brands run their programs here.
**Commission:** Varies by merchant. 5–15% typical.
**Approval:** 3–5 days for the network. Each merchant inside is a separate application.
**Why this matters:** This is where most of the smaller, higher-commission brands live (lacrosse stick companies, swim brands, hockey gear shops, dance apparel).
**What we need from you after approval:** Your ShareASale affiliate ID.

### 4. Bookshop.org
**Apply at:** https://bookshop.org/info/affiliates
**Covers:** Books. Anytime we recommend a parenting or sports-psych book on the site, this becomes the default link instead of Amazon. Supports independent bookstores.
**Commission:** 10% (high for books).
**Approval:** Usually within a week.
**What we need from you after approval:** Your Bookshop affiliate ID.

## Tier 2 — apply next week

Higher commissions on specialty gear. Approval slower. These pay off as the site grows.

### 5. BSN Sports
**Apply at:** https://www.bsnsports.com/ (look for the affiliate or partner link in the footer; some seasons they run through Impact)
**Covers:** Team uniforms, coaching gear, practice equipment. Often the source of school-team gear.
**Commission:** 6–10%.
**Approval:** Slower (10–14 days). They prefer sites with established traffic.

### 6. Soccer.com
**Apply at:** https://www.soccer.com/info/affiliate-program (runs through CJ Affiliate)
**Covers:** Soccer-specific gear. Cleats, balls, shin guards, goalkeeper gloves, training cones.
**Commission:** 5–8%.
**Approval:** 7–14 days through CJ.
**Note:** You'll need a CJ Affiliate account. Apply at https://www.cj.com first.

### 7. BaseballMonkey / SoftballMonkey / HockeyMonkey / LacrosseMonkey
**Apply at:** Each one has its own affiliate page. Run through Avantlink or CJ.
**Covers:** Sport-specific gear deep catalogs. The bat shop, the stick shop, the hockey shop.
**Commission:** 4–8%.
**Approval:** 7–10 days.
**Strategy:** Apply to all four — same family of stores, similar process. Useful when our sport-specific buying guides need precise links to high-end gear.

### 8. Eastbay (Foot Locker family)
**Apply at:** Through Impact Radius.
**Covers:** Athletic apparel, basketball shoes, running gear.
**Commission:** 5–10%.
**Approval:** Variable. Sometimes 2 weeks.

### 9. CJ Affiliate (Commission Junction)
**Apply at:** https://www.cj.com/publisher
**Covers:** Network — gives you access to hundreds of programs including some of the above (Soccer.com, Eastbay).
**Approval:** Network is fast. Each merchant inside is its own application.
**What we need from you:** Your CJ publisher ID.

### 10. Impact Radius
**Apply at:** https://impact.com (publisher signup)
**Covers:** Another network — Dick's, BSN, and many others run their programs here.
**Approval:** Fast for the network, slow for individual merchants.
**What we need from you:** Your Impact partner ID.

## Tier 3 — apply later as the site grows

Smaller programs, niche fits. Worth doing when traffic justifies. Skip these for now.

### 11. Direct programs through specific brands

Once the site has steady monthly visitors, brands will let you in directly:
- **Wilson** (sports balls and equipment) — direct affiliate available
- **Easton** (baseball/softball bats) — direct
- **Rawlings** (gloves) — direct
- **Adidas, Nike** — through their respective programs
- **CamelBak / Hydro Flask** — direct (water bottles)
- **Hyperice / TheraGun** — direct (recovery gear)
- **REI** — has a co-op affiliate program through AvantLink

### 12. Camp + clinic referrals

Some camp directory platforms have referral programs (USCAMPS, ActiveCampaign for sport-specific camps). Most don't. Worth scanning when we have specific camps publishing claim listings.

3. Backfill links across the 100+ articles that mention specific products.

## Practical tips

- **Use one email for all affiliate accounts.** parentcoachplaybook@gmail.com is a good central point.
- **Save the dashboards in a password manager.** Each network has its own login. You'll need to check earnings monthly.
- **Tax forms.** Once approved, networks ask for a W-9. Have it ready.
- **Disclosure required.** Site already has /disclosure/. Each affiliate-link page should reference it.
- **Don't apply to fifteen at once.** Apply in waves so you can respond to questions if any reviewer reaches out.

## What I won't do automatically

- Apply for accounts on your behalf. These need your real identity, payment info, and tax forms.
- Click-fraud accounts up. Each network watches for fake clicks; protect the accounts.
- Bypass Amazon's "you must be a person" rule. Amazon Associates is account-per-person, not per-bot.
